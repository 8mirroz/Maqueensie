# Global benchmark cases

## 1. Caribbean House — Desai Chia Architecture
- Signal: Winner, Best of Year 2025, On the Boards: Single-Family Residential
- Why it matters: elevated tropical residential architecture as experiential living system
- Pattern: climate response + indoor/outdoor continuity + calm monumentality

## 2. Tamarisk House — Steven Harris Architects and RRP
- Signal: Winner, Best of Year 2025, Large Country House
- Why it matters: top-tier benchmark for large residence design
- Pattern: estate-scale privacy, material discipline, high-end detailing, site choreography

## 3. Chapel Residence — NC Design and Architecture
- Signal: Winner, Best of Year 2025, City House
- Why it matters: urban residential benchmark
- Pattern: precise spatial sequencing, quiet luxury, contextual insertion

## 4. Tom Delfim Moreira — Zien, Gensler, Estúdio Figueira
- Signal: Winner, Best of Year 2025, Multi-Unit Housing
- Why it matters: benchmark for branded/luxury multiunit residential
- Pattern: sculptural form, ocean-view choreography, hospitality-like amenities

## 5. Medium Country House by Pitsou Kedem Architects
- Signal: Winner, Best of Year 2025
- Why it matters: monolithic restraint and tactile minimalism
- Pattern: bold exterior / serene interior, natural light control, strong geometry

## 6. Treehouse Apartment — Projekt V Arhitektura
- Signal: completed 2025, featured by ArchDaily in 2026
- Why it matters: family apartment benchmark with sustainable retrofit
- Pattern: natural materials, bespoke child-focused insert, warm minimalism, adaptive reuse

## 7. Franca Apartment — Ana Sawaia Arquitetura
- Signal: completed 2025, featured by ArchDaily
- Why it matters: renovation that preserves previous value while upgrading function
- Pattern: selective preservation, modern refinement, material continuity

# Cross-case patterns

- quiet luxury > loud decorative excess
- tactile natural materials
- sculptural but highly livable planning
- custom furniture and integrated storage
- art curation as identity layer
- site/context specificity
- sustainable retrofit or resilient detailing
