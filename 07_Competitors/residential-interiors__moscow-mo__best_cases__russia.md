# Selected Russian best cases

## 1. AIYA Bureau — Apartment in a historical building, Moscow, 193 m²
- Why selected: clear premium benchmark for Moscow apartment design
- Pattern: historical shell + modern insertions + curated art + calm luxury
- Reusable mechanics:
  - integrate collectible art into the concept
  - work with context instead of erasing it
  - sell “lifestyle reflection” rather than style labels

## 2. Golden Trezzini 2025 residential interior category
- Why selected: current high-signal award pool for Russian apartment/private house interiors
- Pattern: broad range of advanced residential typologies from apartments to private houses
- Reusable mechanics:
  - award-backed trust signal
  - project naming and storytelling
  - portfolio curation through category framing

## 3. Vladimir Afanasev award track
- Why selected: visible recognition in residential interiors — apartments 100+ m² and villas / houses
- Pattern: large-area apartment and villa specialization
- Reusable mechanics:
  - segment specialization
  - awards as positioning proof
  - large-area premium clientele targeting

## 4. Lux Interiors portfolio
- Why selected: consistent design + build + engineering + furniture sourcing proposition
- Pattern: one operator controls project, construction, engineering and furnishings
- Reusable mechanics:
  - guarantees
  - daily supervision
  - procurement savings narrative

# Russian market pattern synthesis

- Premium clients respond to calm luxury, permanence, natural materials, and “timeless” language.
- Moscow premium studios increasingly sell turnkey orchestration and control, not only concept design.
- Trust signals are built via awards, portfolio photography, implementation, guarantees, and supplier access.
